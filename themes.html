Themes

  • The father son relationship
  • In Night the reader sees just how important a strong father-son relationship is
  • Elie and his father manage to help keep each other alive for far longer than the average victim of the holocaust
  • Part of the reason they survive so long is that one is able to watch out for one another, the other, is the desire to survive so as to not leave the other behind
  • This relationship is key in Elie's survival of the holocaust, however in the end his father is not so lucky
  • Silence
  • A major theme in the novel is silence
  • The silence of God is discussed by Elie as he can not believe that if their was a God, how such atrocities would occur
  • Elie had been raised as a devout Jew, yet the sights he sees shuts out all signs of God in his mind
  • The prisoners lack of defiance also represents the theme of silence
  • Nearly all of the prisoners seem to show any willingness to stand up for themselves and many seem resigned to their death, they are silent when they should be resisting
  • Inhumanity
  • This novel tells of the terrible and horrendous acts of the Nazi's
  • During the holocaust the Nazi guards, doctors, and officers committed such acts as mass murders by gas chambers, cremation, and firing squad to name a few
  • Not only did this occur, but many victims were subject to extreme and bizarre medical tests, that were basically torture
  • The Nazi's have seemed to dehumanize the Jewish race in their mind, and therefore show no remorse in their terrible acts as it just comes as second nature
  • They show a lack of humanity, and their acts are thoroughly disgusting
  • Effort to remain faithful during trying times
  • Elie from day one of his experience with Nazi's is subjected to sights of terrible and unjust acts
  • He sees people starving, dying, and suffering daily
  • The atrocious acts he witnesses force him to question whether their really is a God, as why would a God permit such acts
  • He is faced everyday with other Jews who have not given up faith and constantly pray, yet more and more he sees many giving up all faith
  • His struggle to remain faithful ends when he witnesses a young boy being hanged for seemingly no reason
  • Elie reaches a point where he can not see how God exists, and loses his faith
